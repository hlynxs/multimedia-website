HOW PLANETARY AGES WORK

The concept is simple yet profound: your age on another planet is determined by how many times that planet has orbited the Sun during your lifetime. Mercury, with its short 88-day year, would see you celebrating many more birthdays than on Earth. Meanwhile, the outer planets' extended orbits mean you'd age much more slowly by their calendars. This calculator uses precise orbital period data to give you an accurate measure of your solar system age.
